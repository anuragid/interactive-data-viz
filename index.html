A Five-Part Series on Intentional AI Deployment

We Are Choosing
By Not Choosing

The default path of AI automation is already being laid. Each day, organizations make decisions that seem rational in isolation but create consequences no one selected. This series offers frameworks for those who want to choose deliberately: not frameworks against AI, but frameworks for the human judgment that makes deployment wise rather than merely fast.

"Humans access information the model never had."

Ken Holstein, Human-Computer Interaction Institute, Carnegie Mellon University

This single observation anchors everything that follows. AI systems operate under the streetlight. They search where the data is because that is the only place they can search. But the keys are often in the dark: the weight of a pause, the trust built over years, the reasons behind the patterns.

Seven experts from McKinsey, Microsoft AI, Carnegie Mellon, Steelcase, Blink UX, IA Collaborative, and Doblin shared what they see at the highest levels of organizational practice. None oppose AI. All use these tools daily. What they share is a commitment to deploying AI with intention rather than momentum.

What emerges is not a warning against technology, but an invitation: to ask what friction protects, to understand what AI cannot see, to design systems that keep human judgment where it matters, and to honor the pace at which organizations can actually absorb change.
